# Writing Project Proposals

## Overview

Build an original, evidence-grounded response to the current call or client need. Treat authorized successful proposals as comparative evidence—not as templates to copy and never as current authority.

## Establish the Route Before Drafting

1. Identify the exact sponsor/client, program, year, category, stage, submission channel, and intended decision.
2. Obtain the current call/need, official template or system outline, review criteria, attachments, institutional requirements, and deadline.
3. Freeze each source with issuer, version/date, URL or local path, retrieval date, and authority.
4. Build the requirements-response matrix from [requirements-response-matrix.md](assets/requirements-response-matrix.md).
5. List eligibility, limits, duplicate applications, ethics, security, confidentiality, data/IP, partner, budget, signature, seal, and attachment blockers.
6. Choose the applicable route:
   - Read [vertical-projects.md](references/vertical-projects.md) for funding programs.
   - Read [horizontal-projects.md](references/horizontal-projects.md) for commissioned/client work.
7. Verify current official rules online when they may have changed. Do not route from the topic alone.

If the exact program or client requirements are unknown, stop at a route-comparison and gap report. Do not invent a program, duration, budget, scorecard, or submission form.

## Compare Excellent Examples

Before building the proposal argument, read [source-and-case-research.md](references/source-and-case-research.md) and complete [case-comparison-matrix.md](assets/case-comparison-matrix.md).

1. Prefer three to five same-type or structurally comparable successful cases when legitimately available.
2. Record provenance, authorization/license, year, category, success status, and the rules in force at submission.
3. Extract argument structure, evidence strategy, reviewer praise/concerns, and transferable design principles.
4. Mark outdated rules, discipline mismatch, context dependence, and non-transferable content.
5. Never copy wording, figures, data, budgets, layouts, or confidential information.
6. If strong public cases are unavailable, say so and rely on current official criteria plus user-authorized local cases; do not lower the provenance bar.

## Build the Proposal Logic

Use verified project facts to construct this closed loop:

```text
call/client need
  → specific problem and affected decision
  → evidence of importance and gap
  → objectives and testable indicators
  → contents/work packages
  → methods/technical route
  → deliverables and acceptance evidence
  → team/preliminary foundation/resources
  → schedule, risk, and budget
```

For every material claim, record `claim | status | source/evidence | owner | verification date`. Use `MISSING` for unavailable evidence. Proposed objectives and targets must be labeled as proposals and justified by a baseline, method, comparator, and feasibility evidence; never present them as prior results.

## Collaborate Without Fabrication

- Use verified facts and sources only. Never invent publications, references, data, preliminary results, collaborators, approvals, facilities, costs, quotes, or acceptance evidence.
- Keep applicant-authored statements, agent suggestions, and unresolved placeholders distinguishable during drafting.
- Verify every citation against a primary bibliographic source and the cited work itself before final use.
- When a sponsor restricts direct generative-AI authorship, limit work to source retrieval, matrices, questions, outlines, review comments, consistency checks, and other permitted support. Require the responsible applicant to write, verify, disclose, and approve as required.
- Do not claim legal, accounting, eligibility, ethics, or sponsor approval. Route formal decisions to authorized owners.

## Review and Submission Gate

Read [proposal-quality-gates.md](references/proposal-quality-gates.md), then issue findings by severity:

- **Blocker:** ineligible, wrong/outdated form, prohibited AI use, missing authorization, fabricated/unverified claim, unresolved ethics/security/IP issue, unsupported budget, missing required attachment, or inconsistent submission copies.
- **Major:** broken problem-objective-method-indicator logic, no evidence for feasibility, ambiguous acceptance, unowned work, or material schedule/resource risk.
- **Minor:** clarity, structure, terminology, visual, or formatting issue that does not change eligibility or truth.

Do not call a proposal submission-ready until every blocker is resolved by evidence or an authorized human decision.

## Output Contract

Return these sections:

1. Program/client route and controlling sources.
2. Eligibility, format, compliance, and deadline audit.
3. Requirements-response coverage and missing inputs.
4. Excellent-case comparison with provenance and transfer limits.
5. Problem, objectives, indicators, work packages, route, deliverables, evidence, foundation, schedule, risk, and budget logic.
6. Fact/citation verification ledger.
7. Blocker/major/minor review findings.
8. Human confirmations and next actions.

## Common Mistakes

- Selecting a famous program because the topic sounds related.
- Treating successful examples as a fill-in-the-blank application.
- Using an old successful application to override current instructions.
- Inventing precise targets before defining baseline and measurement.
- Hiding missing evidence inside fluent prose.
- Reusing the same argument for a funding review and a client procurement decision.
