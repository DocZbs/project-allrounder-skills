# Source and Case Research

## Source Order

1. Current official call, notice, template/system outline, review criteria, and sponsor/client instructions.
2. Current institutional submission, finance, ethics, security, and IP requirements.
3. User-verified facts and original evidence.
4. Authorized/open successful applications, review summaries, award abstracts, and closeout reports.
5. General writing advice.

Never use level 4 or 5 to override levels 1–3.

## Case Eligibility

Accept a case only when its sponsor/program, year, project type, provenance, success status, and authorization or public-release basis can be recorded. User-authorized internal cases remain local and must not be redistributed.

Reject leaked, paywalled-resale, privacy-invasive, confidential, provenance-unknown, or falsely labeled material.

## Public Starting Points

Use these as discovery starting points, then verify the specific case and current rules:

- [NIH Sample Applications and Documents](https://grants.nih.gov/grants-process/write-application/samples-applications-and-documents) — funded applications and review material released with investigator permission; NIH warns that old forms and instructions may no longer apply.
- [NSF Behavioral and Cognitive Sciences Sample Proposal](https://www.nsf.gov/sbe/bcs/sample-proposal) — a successful proposal released with author permission.
- [NSFC annual notices](https://www.nsfc.gov.cn/) — current calls, application/closeout notices, guides, and management rules; these are rules and public project information, not a blanket source of full winning applications.
- [National Office for Philosophy and Social Sciences](https://www.nopss.gov.cn/) — current calls, forms, management notices, award lists, and public project information.
- [Ministry of Science and Technology](https://www.most.gov.cn/) — current national key R&D management rules and program notices.

## Comparison Method

For each eligible case, extract:

- target reviewer decision and scoring logic;
- problem framing and boundary;
- claim-evidence sequence;
- objective/content/method/indicator alignment;
- preliminary-foundation role;
- uncertainty and risk treatment;
- reviewer praise, concern, or revision signal;
- transferable principle;
- outdated, context-specific, discipline-specific, or protected content that must not transfer.

Write original language from the current project's verified facts after completing the comparison. Never paraphrase sentence-by-sentence from a case.
