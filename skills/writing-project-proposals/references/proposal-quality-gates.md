# Proposal Quality Gates

## Gate 1: Authority and Eligibility

- Current call/client need, template, system outline, deadline, and institution rules are frozen.
- Category, applicant/team eligibility, limits, duplicates, partners, signatures, seals, and attachments are resolved.

## Gate 2: Truth and Provenance

- Every fact, result, citation, facility, person, cost, and commitment is verified.
- Every reference case has provenance and transfer limits.
- Missing evidence remains visible; no generated placeholder looks final.

## Gate 3: Argument Closure

- The problem is specific, consequential, and within scope.
- Objectives answer the problem; indicators measure the objectives.
- Contents and methods are sufficient but not redundant.
- Deliverables and acceptance evidence arise from the work.
- Preliminary foundation supports feasibility without being overstated.
- Schedule, team, resources, risk, and budget support the same plan.

## Gate 4: Reviewer/Client Decision

Each section helps the intended decision-maker answer: Why this problem? Why now? Why this route? Why this team? What will be delivered? How will success be verified? What can fail and how is it controlled?

## Gate 5: Consistency

Reconcile title, terminology, objectives, numbers, dates, work packages, indicators, team roles, budget, figures, attachments, online fields, PDF, and printed copies. Treat any material mismatch as a blocker.

## Severity Output

For each finding record `severity | location | requirement | observed issue | evidence | remediation | owner | status`. Lead with blockers, then major and minor findings.
