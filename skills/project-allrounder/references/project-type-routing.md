# Project-Type Routing

Always read the user's current documents first. The routes below identify questions, not permanent rules.

## Vertical Projects

### Natural-science funding

Prioritize current call/template, eligibility and limits, research question, objectives, novelty, research content, approach, preliminary evidence, feasibility, ethics/data, budget, annual/progress obligations, and outcome reporting. Verify the current funder's generative-AI rules before assisting with formal text.

### Social-science and humanities funding

Prioritize topic scope, disciplinary route, research object, conceptual contribution, literature and evidence base, methodology, schedule, expected outputs, attribution, appraisal/identification, change approval, and closeout rules.

### Key R&D and mission-oriented programs

Prioritize call indicators, topic/task hierarchy, consortium roles, milestones, performance metrics, engineering demonstration, data/reporting duties, fund allocation, risk, and task-level acceptance evidence.

### Provincial, municipal, and institutional programs

Treat the current local call, form, online-system outline, institution notice, and finance rules as controlling. Map local requirements onto the common master record without assuming national-program rules apply.

## Horizontal Projects

Prioritize signed scope, client problem, requirement baseline, deliverables, acceptance tests, payment triggers, tax/invoice duties, reimbursable costs, IP/background IP, data rights, confidentiality, subcontracting, publication rights, warranty/support, change procedure, termination, handover, and dispute escalation.

Never turn commercial or legal observations into a formal legal conclusion. Flag clauses for the user's authorized legal, finance, or contract owner.

## Mixed Projects

Keep separate authority columns for program requirements and client/partner contracts. When they conflict, record the conflict and obtain an authorized resolution; do not merge them into an invented compromise.
