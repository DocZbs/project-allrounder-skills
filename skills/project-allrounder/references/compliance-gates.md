# Compliance Gates

## Source Precedence

Resolve conflicts in this order, while recording every conflict:

1. Current call, special notice, online-system outline, or signed agreement governing the exact task.
2. Approved task book, contract amendment, formal approval, or budget approval.
3. Current institutional, sponsor, and finance rules.
4. Verified facts and original evidence approved by the responsible owner.
5. Historical examples and general practice.

## Formal-Output Gate

Before a submission, report, deck, invoice/reimbursement package, acceptance package, handover, or closeout:

- confirm the current template and submission channel;
- trace every material claim and number to a source or evidence path;
- verify citations and external facts against primary sources;
- resolve or disclose eligibility, ethics, data, confidentiality, IP, and security issues;
- reconcile scope, dates, indicators, deliverables, budget, and versions;
- identify required human review, signature, seal, approval, or transmission;
- keep a final audit record and submitted-file hash/version when permitted.

## Generative-AI Gate

Check the sponsor's current official policy for the specific year and program. Where AI-generated formal text is prohibited or restricted, limit assistance to rule retrieval, source organization, question lists, evidence matrices, outlines, review comments, and verification support. Require the applicant or responsible author to write, verify, disclose, and accept responsibility as required.

## Finance Gate

Do not assert that an expense is allowable or reimbursable without the current budget, voucher, purpose, approval status, and institutional rule. Present a review checklist and exceptions; leave formal approval to authorized finance staff.

## External-Action Gate

Do not sign, submit, pay, approve, contact stakeholders, disclose confidential content, or change permissions without explicit user authority and any required confirmation.
