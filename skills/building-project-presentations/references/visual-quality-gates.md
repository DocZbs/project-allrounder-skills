# Visual and Delivery Quality Gates

Follow the installed `presentations` skill when its current requirements are stricter.

## Content Gate

- Audience, decision, duration, and required topics are covered.
- Each slide has one dominant claim and advances the story.
- Every material number and conclusion is traceable.
- Required indicators/deliverables are mapped to evidence.
- No fabricated outcome, source, quotation, image, or acceptance judgment.

## Visual Gate

- Inspect every rendered slide at full size.
- No unintended overlap, off-canvas object, clipped text, overflow, title wrap, broken connector, distorted image, or cropped axis/legend/panel label.
- Main evidence is readable at presentation scale.
- Text is shortened or split before reducing size below the presentation skill's minimums.
- Layout rhythm varies naturally; no repeated card grid or decorative metric row used merely to fill space.
- Figures, captions, sources, and callouts remain visually associated.
- Template typography, palette, spacing, footer, and page markers remain consistent when a template is supplied.

## Delivery Gate

- Final deliverable exists as `.pptx` and reopens successfully.
- Programmatic slide bounds/overflow tests pass.
- Every slide is rendered; changed slides are re-rendered after fixes.
- Speaker notes are present and useful where planned.
- Planned speaking time fits the requested duration.
- Expert-question bank targets evidence gaps and likely reviewer concerns.
- Output contains no unresolved placeholder label or accidental production note.

## Severity

**High:** unsupported/fabricated claim, missing central evidence, wrong conclusion, overlap, cut-off text, unreadable key evidence, broken/missing slide, or material data mismatch. Block delivery.

**Medium:** excessive density, weak source attribution, detached caption, repetitive synthetic layout, poor crop, missing notes, weak transition, or timing overrun. Fix when feasible before delivery.

**Low:** minor alignment, spacing, palette, or optional refinement that does not affect comprehension.

Record remaining limitations honestly; never call a deck visually verified when full-size renders were unavailable.
